Juju Dhau

Juju Dhau is a sweetened custard-like yogurt that is mostly centred at Kathmandu valley, Nepal, and is an important element of all feasts and celebrations specially in the Newar community. In the Newari language Juju Dhau literally means "king of yoghurt". While cow's milk is used to make regular yoghurt. The richer taste and texture of juju dhau is the result of using fresh buffalo milk.

The texture and taste of Juju Dhau is which makes it adorable. It is prepared in a traditional clay pot which is known as kataura. The clay pot helps in the loss of moisture and gives a firm texture to it. Instead of sugar, spices like, coconuts, cardamom, etc are added to it to create mouth-melting sweetness. Like other yoghurt, Juju Dhau is also created by bacterial fermentation, which is a metabolic process that changes sugar into acids, liquor and gasses. It happens in yeast and in microorganisms.

The culture mostly used for Juju dhau includes Lactobacillus delbrueki subsp. Bulgaricus, Streptococcus thermophilus, lactobacillus, and Bifidobacterium. Juju Dhau may be viewed as natural healing food supplement for intestinal microflora. It has a perfect balance of protein, fat, carbohydrates, minerals and vitamins.
